##  Increasing In-Store Product Visibility for Apparel Brands with Retailer Incentives 

![Image](/images/blog/Girls-choosing-modern-clothes-in-store-flat-vector-illustration-scaled.jpg) In the crowded apparel market, ensuring your products stand out on store shelves can make a massive difference to your bottom line. Consumers are often bombarded with choices, and it’s usually the products that catch their eye first that end up in their shopping carts. In-store visibility plays a crucial role in determining whether your brand gets noticed and ultimately purchased. However, gaining premium in-store space requires more than just sending your products to retailers. By offering the right **incentives** , you can encourage retailers to prioritize your brand, giving your apparel prime positioning and more opportunities to drive sales.

These incentives can be structured around specific outcomes, such as product placement, sales volume, or special promotions. Here are some **incentive strategies** that can help boost your apparel brand’s visibility: 

* * *

**1\. Incentivizing Key Shelf Space and Window Displays** In retail, the positioning of your products matters. Apparel displayed at eye level, near the entrance, or on mannequins is far more likely to be noticed and purchased. Offering retailers **extra rewards** for placing your clothing in these high-traffic areas can make all the difference. **Strategy Tip:**

  * **Bonus for Window Displays** : Offer an additional reward for retailers that feature your products on mannequins or in window displays for a set period. For example, you could offer a **cash bonus** or a discount on future orders for any retailer that keeps your apparel as the central feature of their front-window display. 

**Example:**   
A fashion brand incentivizes retailers to display its latest collection in the front window. Retailers that maintain the display for two weeks receive a monetary bonus, and if they reach specific sales targets within that period, they unlock a **tiered reward** system. **2\. Co-Branding to Boost In-Store Promotions** One of the most effective ways to get apparel noticed in stores is through **co-branded promotions** . By offering exclusive promotions that feature your brand in collaboration with the retailer, you create a sense of partnership and shared success. Retailers are more likely to push your products when they see the potential for increased traffic and sales. 

  * **Exclusive Co-Branded Promotions** : Partner with retailers to create limited-time offers or promotions featuring your products. For example, offer exclusive discounts on your apparel during the retailer’s seasonal sales events, where your brand and the store’s name both feature prominently in the promotional material. 

An apparel brand collaborates with a major retailer to run a **Diwali co-branded promotion** . Retailers that participate receive promotional support and bonuses based on the volume of sales generated during the festive season. The brand provides banners, in-store signage, and digital marketing support to drive traffic to the store. **3\. Rewards for Special Themed Sections** Setting up **themed sections** or dedicated zones in a store creates more visibility and increases the likelihood of customer engagement. Whether it’s a seasonal collection or a special promotion, encouraging retailers to create special areas in the store for your brand can lead to higher sales. 

  * **Seasonal Zones and Themes** : Offer additional rewards for retailers who create themed zones for your apparel line during seasonal promotions or events. For example, during the back-to-school shopping season, retailers could create a "Back-to-School" section featuring your brand, and you could offer bonuses based on the number of sales generated from that section. 

A retailer sets up a **Summer Collection** section featuring swimwear and beach apparel from your brand. For every $10,000 in sales generated from that section, the retailer earns a bonus or an extra discount on their next wholesale order. **4\. Gamifying Product Placement with Visual Merchandising Contests** Visual merchandising plays a huge role in how customers perceive a brand in-store. Encouraging retailers to get creative with their displays can increase brand awareness and attract customers. By running a visual merchandising contest, you can create competition among retailers to put your products in the spotlight. 

  * **Visual Merchandising Challenges** : Run a competition where retailers submit photos of their in-store displays featuring your products, and reward the best or most creative setups. This not only increases visibility but also engages retailers in a fun, competitive way. 

An apparel brand runs a contest where retailers are asked to create the most eye-catching window display featuring their new collection. The retailer with the most creative display wins a weekend getaway, while other participants receive branded merchandise or bonus points. **5\. Offering Early Access or Exclusive Lines for Prime Placement** Retailers are more likely to push products that they can offer **exclusively** or before other stores. Giving your top-performing retailers early access to new collections or exclusive lines can motivate them to give your brand the prime placement it deserves. 

  * **Early Access Rewards** : Reward retailers who provide your apparel with prominent display areas by giving them early access to your next collection or offering them exclusive rights to limited-edition lines. 

A fashion brand rewards retailers who consistently place their products in front-window displays with **early access** to the upcoming fall collection. Retailers that meet sales targets during the exclusivity period are given the option to sell the collection two weeks before the general launch. **6\. Real-Time Rewards to Keep Retailers Engaged** The quicker retailers see the rewards from their efforts, the more motivated they’ll be to continue. Offering **real-time rewards** for product placement and sales helps ensure that retailers remain engaged and focused on driving sales for your brand.
